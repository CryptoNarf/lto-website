LTO Network can be used by any company with a need for a production-ready blockchain that allows for digital verification of digital assets and enabling collaboration through the use of decentralized workflows.

Timeline & Roadmap

2014

2014 - Q4

LegalThings is incorporated

LegalThings is created as a spin-off of FIRM24. LegalThings provides a software suite for building documents and digital signing.

2016

2016 - Q1

Workflow engine v1.0

The LegalThings workflow engine is released. The first version powers the FIRM24 procedures for incorporations. Others clients start using the engine for digitizing the contracts as FSM (Finite State Machine) and complex signing procedures.

2017

2017 - Q3

Hackathon winner by the Dutch Ministry of Justice

During the blockchain hackathon organized by the Dutch Ministry of Justice and Security, we demonstrated that a whole legal procedure can be digitized as an FSM and stored on the blockchain. This got us the 1st prize in the event which included the assignment to create the first digitized legal procedures for the Dutch legislative system on the blockchain.

2018

2018- Q1

Launch of LTO Network & seed sale

LegalThings becomes LTO Network. The private layer is released, depending on Waves as public layer, featuring a modified version of the workflow engine. $1.4MM was raised during the seed sale.

2018 - Q3

Pilot for Waste Transportation

LTO launches a decentralized solution for automated permit checks in assignment of the Dutch and Belgian ministry of interiors. This marks the first time LTO Network is used in a production environment.

2019

2019 - Q1

Mainnet launch and crowd sale

The LTO Network mainnet is launched; a modified fork of Waves. We close the last round of token sale, raising almost $1MM from the crowd sale and $1.6MM from the private sale. LTO is listed on BitMax.

2019 - Q3

NEN preferred blockchain

LTO Network becomes the preferred blockchain of the NEN; The Royal Dutch institute for normalization and standards.

2020

2020 - Q1

Binance lists LTO Network

LTO Network is selected by Binance for free listing. Initially only ERC20 and BEP2 are supported and Binance now have LTO mainnet tokens available. LTO is also listed by LiteBit and BitMax.

2020 - Q2

fillthedoc & LetsFlow

fillthedoc is launched, a standalone product that anchors on LTO Network. The workflow engine is rebranded as LetsFlow and separated from the LTO private layer.

2020 - Q2

Associations and sponsored accounts

The first major mainnet upgrade, adding two transaction types: associations and sponsored accounts.

2020 - Q2

Governance improvement,🔥 burning 50M LTO

LTO Network burns all 50 million tokens held by the foundation, which is 11% of the total supply.

2020 - Q3

Staking on Binance and BitMax

Exchanges Binance and BitMax launch locked staking programs for LTO as alternative to staking and leasing on mainnet using a community node.

2020 - Q3

Deflationary token economics

With the second major mainnet update, LTO Network switches to a deflationary model. For every transaction, part of the fee is burned.

2020 - Q4

United Nations land registry

United Nations and LTO Network release world’s first open-source land registry. The land registry solution is handed to the Afghan Government by the UN Human Settlements Programme Afghanistan.

2020 - Q4

LTOxVIDT merger

LTO Network and VIDT Datalink join forces to become the front-to-back B2B blockchain solution. Both tokens will continue to exists and serve different purposes.

2021

2021 - Q1

100.000 daily transactions

LTO Network reaches the milestone of 100.000 daily transactions, becoming the 12th most used public blockchain according to Coinstats.

UPCOMING

2021 - Q1

LTO Identity platform

Launch of the LTO Identity platform, supporting Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs).

2021 - Q2

Public certificate support on mainnet

New transaction type on mainnet, allowing organizations to publish X.509 certificates on-chain. LTO mainnet will support addition cryptographic algorithms like EdDSA and RSA.

2021 - Q2

Cross-chain DIDs

The identity platform will be able to generate and index DIDs of other blockchains, like Ethereum, based on the public key from a published public certificate.

2021 - Q3

Cross-chain associations

LTO can be used by DeFi projects to meet KYC regulations, partnering with Chainlink as oracle network.

2021 - Q4

Identity workflows

The LTO private layer can be used in combination with the identity platform to give individuals full control over their personal data. Projects can meet GDPR requirements.

2022

2022

LTO Network v3 - DeFi

LTO Network enters the DeFi space, bridging the gap between traditional finance and crypto.
